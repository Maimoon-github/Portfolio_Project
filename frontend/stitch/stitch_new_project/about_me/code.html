Personal Story

My journey didn't start with a keyboard, but with a sketchbook. I was obsessed with how physical spaces influenced human behavior—the flow of a gallery, the acoustics of a cathedral. When I discovered the web in 2012, I didn't see just websites; I saw virtual architecture.

Over the last decade, I've transitioned from a self-taught enthusiast to a Senior Architect, but that original obsession with "Atmospheric Depth" remains. I believe the best interfaces are the ones that feel protective—where the user never feels lost, and every interaction feels intentional.

When I'm not designing component APIs or optimizing build pipelines, you'll find me exploring brutalist architecture or analog photography, finding inspiration in the grain of a physical print.
